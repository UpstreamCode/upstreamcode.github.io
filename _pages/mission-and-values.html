About Us

Mission & Values

Bringing Digital Scripture Engagement to unreached language groups — that people of all nations, tribes, and tongues might know the grace of God in our Lord Jesus Christ.

Who We Are

Upstream is the vehicle by which a community of technologists, designers, and church planters innovate together.

What We Do

We partner with missional technologists and field teams to meaningfully impact church planting efforts to the unreached.

How We Do It

Through volunteers and intern cohorts, we build apps that distribute digital Scripture, story, and song to unreached language groups.

Our Values

In addition to Pioneers’ values, Upstream has some further emphases. They inform both the way we work and the things we produce.

Theological and Missiological Reflection

In contrast to some recent trends in modern missions, we do not view speed or reproducibility as the highest aims in church planting efforts. Our ecclesiology and ministry philosophy serve as the foundation for how we approach evangelism and discipleship.

People Over Product

Relationships matter. We strive to be personable, accessible, and committed to success together.

Quality Products for the Least of These

We care about bringing excellence and delight to our end users (missionary teams and unreached language groups).

Our Partners

We regularly serve alongside these organizations.

There are many ways to grow in understanding the unreached. It can be an overwhelming and confusing experience for some. For example, many different terms can be found in the missions world to articulate slightly different concepts, such as, “unreached language groups”, “unengaged unreached people groups”, “frontier people groups”, “least reached peoples”, “ethno-linguistic groups”...the list goes on!

To clear this up and get you started on your journey, here are some places we’d point you to:

  1. Perspectives – If you’re in a country with a course nearby, we’d encourage you to look at joining.
  2. Joshua Project and People Groups – We’d encourage you to begin by praying for the unreached. These two resources are great for exploring particular unreached groups and knowing how to pray for them. Joshua Project Definitions is a great page for disentangling all of the jargon associated with the unreached world.
